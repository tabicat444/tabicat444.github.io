About Me

I am fascinated by the overlap of social services, data science, and law. I am an aspiring immigration lawyer and intend to research the positive impacts of immigration and refugee resettlement on local communities -- hopefully within California. I recently graduated as a first-generation college student from Princeton University; I've been spending time since reconnecting with my home community. I worked at my former high school, Orange Glen, supporting graduating seniors applying to college during COVID-19 and virtual learning. Currently, I'm serving as an AmeriCorps VIP (Volunteer Infrastructure Project) Fellow at Jewish Family Service's Refugee Resettlement Agency.

Social Services

I am an active Crisis Counselor at Crisis Text Line as well as a certified Domestic Violence Counselor in the State of California. Serving my AmeriCorps term in a refugee resettlement agency has been inspiring; clients, staff, and volunteers have reaffirmed my believe that social services are critical to the longterm success of local communities.

Data Science

I had never coded before college and was actually worried that I was going to break my first laptop by coding in R. My interest in quantitative analysis really began in 2016 as a college freshman: I watched professors grapple with the shock of the 2016 election that the quantitative social science community failed to predict. As a very new first-generation college student, seeing professors make such a glaring mistake really stayed with me. I found it captivating that there was so much room for growth. Over 5 years later, I have graduated with a minor in Statistics and Machine Learning, co-authored a publication on data science teaching pedagogy, freelance as an R tutor and consultant, and am pursuing IBM's Data Science Professional Certificate.

Law

I believe that law's involvement in most people's lives is largely indirect. Most people are not cognizant of regulations dictating pieces of their lives. The people who are often the most aware are those whose legal statuses or rights are regularly debated, like refugees, asylees, and immigrants. Growing up in a border community shaped my beliefs and serving with Refugee Resettlement have strengthened them. At Princeton, my research focused on misconduct within US Border Protection and how the Trump Administration's Zero-Tolerance Policy negatively impacted the lives of children.
